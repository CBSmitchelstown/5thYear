History of Sampdoria

U.C. Sampdoria was founded in 1946 through the merger of two clubs: Sampierdarenese and Andrea Doria, both based in Genoa, Italy.

The club’s most successful era came in the late 1980s and early 1990s. Under manager Vujadin Boškov, Sampdoria won their only Serie A title in 1990–91.

They also claimed 4 Coppa Italia titles and reached the European Cup final in 1992, narrowly losing to Barcelona at Wembley.

Sampdoria plays at the historic Stadio Luigi Ferraris, which they share with local rivals Genoa C.F.C.

Despite recent challenges, the club remains a beloved symbol of Genoa’s football tradition.
